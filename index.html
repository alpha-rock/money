Coinages of India

1980 - 2019

A visualization of changes in the design of coins and amount
of raw material used to mint coins of India

Abstract

Currency, coin and paper money, are strong representation of the culture and development of the nation. Hence the design of the coin and material it is made from keeps constantly changing. Chronologically, the main considerations influencing the coinage size and shape over time have been:

  • Coinage Reforms with the introduction of the metric system
  • The need felt from time to time to obviate the possibility of the metallic value of coins rising beyond the face value. The cost-benefit of coinisation of currency notes


    • What did I do?

    Data gathering

    I started with collecting data on the size and major design changes happened on coins during the last few decades. Followed by collecting data on the increase in prices of metal used to make the following coins.


    Data processing and Analysis

    Gathered data was then used to calculate the amount of metal used to mint coins of each denomination. Data of changes in the shape of the coin were mapped on prices of metal used to make those coins
